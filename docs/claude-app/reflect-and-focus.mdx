On **July 9, 2026**, Claude added two quietly personal features: **Reflect** (a monthly recap of how you use Claude) and **Time and focus** settings (break reminders and quiet hours). They're small in surface area but worth understanding, because they sit on top of your **memory** and shape how much of your usage Claude analyzes about you.

## What Reflect shows you

Reflect lives at **Settings → Reflect**. Once a month it surfaces:

- the **topics you spent time on**,
- your **most active day and peak hour**, and
- **observations about how you work** with Claude.

It's in **beta on Free, Pro, and Max**, on the **web and Claude Desktop**.

<Callout type="warning">
Reflect **requires [memory](/docs/claude-app/memory) to be enabled.** That's the key dependency: the recap is generated from what Claude retains about your conversations. No memory, no recap — and turning memory on to get the recap means Claude is also carrying facts about you across chats. Decide on the memory trade-off first; the recap is downstream of it.
</Callout>

## Time and focus: break reminders and quiet hours

Separately, **Settings → Time and focus** lets you configure **optional break reminders** and **quiet hours** to manage your own work patterns. These are nudges you opt into — a reminder to step away, and windows where Claude won't prompt you.

## The trade-off worth naming

Usage-insight features are useful and also, by design, **engagement-shaped**: a recap that celebrates your "most active day" is a mirror, but it can also be a nudge to come back. Reporting on Reflect's launch framed it as *quietly* encouraging more AI use. That's not a reason to avoid it — it's a reason to read it deliberately.

<Callout type="tip">
A healthy way to use Reflect: skim the *topics* to notice where your attention actually went, ignore the streak-and-peak framing, and let quiet hours — not a recap — decide when you step away. The insight is the value; the gamification is optional.
</Callout>

## How this relates to other memory features

Don't confuse the distinct things Claude calls "memory-adjacent":

| Feature | What it does | Needs memory on? |
|---|---|---|
| [Memory](/docs/claude-app/memory) | Carries facts about you across chats | — (it *is* memory) |
| **Reflect** | Monthly recap of topics, peak time, work observations | **Yes** |
| **Time and focus** | Break reminders and quiet hours | No |
| [Projects](/docs/claude-app/projects) | Scoped context for a body of work | No |
